_Lawn in front of the terrace at Hunstanton_.

ACT I. _The Terrace at Hunstanton Chase_.

[SIR JOHN _and_ LADY CAROLINE PONTEFRACT, MISS WORSLEY, _on chairs under large yew tree_.]

LADY CAROLINE.

I believe this is the first English country house you have stayed at, Miss Worsley?

HESTER.

Yes, Lady Caroline.

LADY CAROLINE.

You have no country houses, I am told, in America?

HESTER.

We have not many.

LADY CAROLINE.

Have you any country? What we should call country?

HESTER.

[_Smiling_.] We have the largest country in the world, Lady Caroline. They used to tell us at school that some of our states are as big as France and England put together.

LADY CAROLINE.

SIR JOHN.
